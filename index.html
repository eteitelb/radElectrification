Does your heat pump beat gas
at the coldest hour?

Combining EIA 2024 energy prices with ASHRAE 99% heating design temperatures: a state-by-state view of whether an air-source heat pump saves money on fuel even under worst-case winter conditions.

How this works: Jenkins' break-even COP tells you what efficiency a heat pump needs to beat gas on fuel cost: break-even COP = (elec ¢/kWh ÷ gas ¢/kWhth) × AFUE. This map adds climate reality — computing the actual COP your heat pump achieves at the coldest design temperature (real COP = η × Thot/(Thot−Tcold)), then asks: is real COP ≥ break-even COP? A ratio > 1.0 means the heat pump wins on fuel cost even at its worst operating moment. A ratio < 1.0 means the economics flip during the coldest hours — though annual average performance will still favor the heat pump in most cases.

New Radiant system bonus — air temperature setback via comfort model

A radiant heating panel elevates mean radiant temperature (MRT), allowing air temperature to be set back while maintaining the same operative temperature comfort level. Per ASHRAE 55, Top = 0.5·Tair + 0.5·TMRT, so each 1°F MRT boost above setpoint enables a 1°F air setback. Lower air temperature reduces transmission losses and improves heat pump COP — both compounding the energy savings.

Model source: Teitelbaum, Jayathissa, Miller & Meggers, "Design with Comfort: Expanding the psychrometric chart with radiation and convection dimensions," Energy and Buildings 209 (2020).
